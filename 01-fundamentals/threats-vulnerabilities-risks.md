# Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Risks

## Overview

Threats, vulnerabilities, and risks are fundamental cybersecurity concepts.

Many beginners confuse these terms, but understanding the differences is essential.

---

# Threat

## Definition

A threat is anything that has the potential to cause harm to a system, network, application, or organization.

Threats can be intentional or accidental.

## Examples

- Hackers
- Malware
- Phishing attacks
- Insider threats
- Power outages
- Natural disasters

## Example

A hacker attempting to gain unauthorized access to a web application is a threat.

---

# Vulnerability

## Definition

A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.

A vulnerability does not automatically cause damage, but it creates an opportunity for an attack.

## Examples

- Weak passwords
- Unpatched software
- Misconfigured servers
- SQL Injection flaws
- Missing access controls

## Example

Using "admin123" as a password is a vulnerability.

---

# Risk

## Definition

Risk is the possibility that a threat will successfully exploit a vulnerability and cause damage.

Risk depends on:

- The threat
- The vulnerability
- The potential impact

## Formula

Risk can be thought of as:

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact

## Example

Threat:
A hacker.

Vulnerability:
A weak password.

Impact:
Unauthorized access to sensitive data.

Combined together, they create risk.

---

# Practical Example

Imagine a school website.

## Threat

A malicious attacker.

## Vulnerability

Outdated login system with weak password requirements.

## Risk

The attacker may gain access to student information.

---

# Types of Threats

## Human Threats

- Hackers
- Insider threats
- Social engineering
- Phishing

## Technical Threats

- Malware
- Viruses
- Worms
- Ransomware

## Environmental Threats

- Fire
- Flooding
- Power outages

---

# Reducing Risk

Organizations reduce risk by:

- Fixing vulnerabilities
- Implementing security controls
- Monitoring systems
- Training users
- Performing regular updates

---

# Key Takeaway

Threat:
Something that can cause harm.

Vulnerability:
A weakness that can be exploited.

Risk:
The likelihood and impact of harm occurring when a threat exploits a vulnerability.

Understanding these concepts is critical because most cybersecurity work focuses on identifying vulnerabilities and reducing risk.
